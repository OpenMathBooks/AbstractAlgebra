\question Find the degrees of field extensions, and briefly explain (using polynomials).\\  Then give a basis for each larger field over the smaller field. 
\begin{parts}
\part $[\Q(\sqrt{2}):\Q]$
\begin{solution}
The degree is 2.  The minimum polynomial for $\sqrt{2}$ over $\Q$ is $x^2 - 2$, which is irreducible and has degree 2.  A basis is $\{1, \sqrt{2}\}$.
\end{solution}
\vfill
\part $[\Q(\sqrt{2},\sqrt[3]{5+\sqrt{2}}):\Q(\sqrt{2})]$
\begin{solution}
The degree is 3.  A minimum polynomial for $\sqrt[3]{5+\sqrt{2}}$ over $\Q(\sqrt{2})$ is $x^3 - 5 - \sqrt{2}$.  To prove this is the minimum polynomial we need to show it is irreducible, which takes a little work.  
%Note that if the polynomial did factor, it would have a root.  That root would be of the form $a+b\sqrt{2}$.  Substituting this in to the polynomial gives
%\[(a+b\sqrt{2})^3 - 5 - \sqrt{2} = a^3 + 3a^2b\sqrt{2} + 6ab^2 + 2b^3\sqrt{2} - 5 - \sqrt{2}\]
%If this is zero, then $a^3 + 6ab^2 - 5 = 0$ and $3a^2b + 2b^2 - 1 = 0$

The basis is $\{1, \sqrt[3]{5+\sqrt{2}}, \sqrt[3]{5+\sqrt{2}}^2\}$.
\end{solution}
\vfill
\part $[\Q(\sqrt{2},\sqrt[3]{5+\sqrt{2}}):\Q(\sqrt[3]{5+\sqrt{2}})]$
\begin{solution}
The degree is 1.  Since $\sqrt[3]{5+\sqrt{2}}^3 = 5+\sqrt{2}$ and $5$ is in $\Q$, we have that $\sqrt{2}$ is already in $\Q(\sqrt[3]{5+\sqrt{2}})$.  The minimum polynomial is $x - \sqrt{2}$.  A basis is $\{1\}$
\end{solution}
\vfill
\part $[\Q(\sqrt[3]{5+\sqrt{2}}): \Q]$
\begin{solution}
The degree is 6.  Since $\Q(\sqrt[3]{5+\sqrt{2}}) = \Q(\sqrt{2}, \sqrt[3]{5+\sqrt{2}})$ (as seen in part (c)), we can iterate the extensions using parts (a) and (b).  In class we prove that $[E:F] = [E:L][L:F]$, so in this case we get the degree to be $2\cdot 3  = 6$.

The minimum polynomial is $x^6 - 10x^3 + 23$.  

A basis is $\{1, \sqrt[3]{5+\sqrt{2}}, \sqrt[3]{5+\sqrt{2}}^2, \sqrt[3]{5+\sqrt{2}}^3, \sqrt[3]{5+\sqrt{2}}^4, \sqrt[3]{5+\sqrt{2}}^5\}$.
\end{solution}
\vfill
\end{parts}
