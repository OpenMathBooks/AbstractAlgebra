Pick up where we left off when factoring of \(\C\text{.}\) Then multiply the two factors corresponding to the complex conjugate roots. We find

\begin{equation*} (x-\sqrt[3]{5}e^{i\pi/3})(x-\sqrt[3]{5}e^{i5\pi/3}) - x^2 -\sqrt[3]{5}e^{i5\pi/3}x - \sqrt[3]{5}e^{i\pi/3}x + \sqrt[3]{5}^2e^{i6\pi/3}\text{.} \end{equation*}

The last term is just \(\sqrt[3]{5}^2\text{.}\) This makes sense if you think about adding the angles.
