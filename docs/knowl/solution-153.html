Solution 11.3.1.3.1.

Let \(H = \langle 3 \rangle = \{0, 3, 6, 9\}\) and \(K = \langle 4 \rangle = \{0, 4, 8\}\text{.}\) The intersection \(H \cap K = \{0\}\) and every element in \(\Z_{12}\) can be written as the sum of something from \(H\) and something from \(K\text{.}\)

Notice two things that will be relevant shortly: \(\langle 3 \rangle \cong \Z_4\) and \(\langle 4 \rangle \cong \Z_3\text{,}\) and those two groups were the parts of the direct product version of \(\Z_{12}\text{.}\) Second, notice that the elements in \(\langle 3 \rangle\) all have order \(2^k\) for some \(k\text{,}\) and the elements of \(\langle 4 \rangle\) have order \(3^k\) for some \(k\) (here just \(k = 0\) and \(k=1\)). How very curious.
