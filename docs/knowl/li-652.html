Item 11.3.4.1.a.

The only primes which divide \(|U(35)|\) are 2 and 3, so we are looking for a \(2\)-group and a \(3\)-group. We take \(G(2) = \{1,6,8,13,22, 27, 29, 34\}\) to be the group of elements whose orders are powers of \(2\) and \(G(3) = \{1, 11, 16\}\) to be the group of elements whose orders are powers of 3. Clearly \(G(2) \cap G(3) = \{1\}\text{,}\) and we can also show that \(G(2)\cdot G(3) = U(35)\) by simply multiplying out every pair of elements, one from \(G(2)\) and the other from \(G(3)\) (we will get exactly the elements of \(U(35)\)).
