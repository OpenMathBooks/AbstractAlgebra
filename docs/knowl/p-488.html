Yes, \(H\) is a subgroup of \(G\text{.}\) For closure under addition, consider \((x_1, y_1) \in H\) and \((x_2, y_2) \in H\text{.}\) This means that \(y_1 = 2x_1\) and \(y_2 = 2x_2\text{.}\) What about \((x_1+x_2, y_1+y_2)\text{?}\) Well, \(y_1+y_2 = 2x_1 + 2x_2 = 2(x_1+x_2)\text{,}\) so indeed, \((x_1+x_2, y_1+y_2) \in H\text{.}\)
