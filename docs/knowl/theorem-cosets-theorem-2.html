Theorem 3.4.

Let \(H\) be a subgroup of a group \(G\text{.}\) Then the left cosets of \(H\) in \(G\) partition \(G\text{.}\) That is, the group \(G\) is the disjoint union of the left cosets of \(H\) in \(G\text{.}\)

Proof.

Let \(g_1 H\) and \(g_2 H\) be two cosets of \(H\) in \(G\text{.}\) We must show that either \(g_1 H \cap g_2 H = \emptyset\) or \(g_1 H = g_2 H\text{.}\) Suppose that \(g_1 H \cap g_2 H \neq \emptyset\) and \(a \in g_1 H \cap g_2 H\text{.}\) Then by the definition of a left coset, \(a = g_1 h_1 = g_2 h_2\) for some elements \(h_1\) and \(h_2\) in \(H\text{.}\) Hence, \(g_1 = g_2 h_2 h_1^{-1}\) or \(g_1 \in g_2 H\text{.}\) By LemmaÂ 3.3, \(g_1 H = g_2 H\text{.}\)
