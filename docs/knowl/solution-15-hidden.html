The identity of \(G\) is \(0\text{,}\) and \(0 = \log 1\text{.}\) Since \(1 \in \Q\) and positive, we see that \(0 \in H\text{.}\)

Suppose \(a, b \in H\text{.}\) This means that \(a = \log x\) and \(b = \log y\) for some \(x,y \in \Q\text{,}\) both positive. What about \(a+b\text{?}\) Well, \(a+b = \log x + \log y = \log(xy)\text{.}\) We have \(xy \in \Q\) and positive, so \(a+b \in H\text{.}\)

Finall, for inverses, suppose \(a \in H\text{.}\) Then \(a = \log x\) for positive \(x \in \Q\text{.}\) The inverse will be \(-a = -\log x = \log(x\inv)\text{.}\) But \(x\inv \in \Q\) and still positive, so \(-a \in H\text{.}\)
