Proposition 9.22.

Let \(E\) be a splitting field over \(F\) of a separable polynomial. Then \(E_{G(E/F)} = F\text{.}\)

Proof.

Let \(G = G(E/F)\text{.}\) Clearly, \(F \subset E_G \subset E\text{.}\) Also, \(E\) must be a splitting field of \(E_G\) and \(G(E/F) = G(E/E_G)\text{.}\) By TheoremÂ 9.15,

\begin{equation*} |G| = [E: E_G] =[ E:F]\text{.} \end{equation*}

Therefore, \([E_G : F ] =1\text{.}\) Consequently, \(E_G = F\text{.}\)
