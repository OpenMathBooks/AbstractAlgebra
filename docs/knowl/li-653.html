Item 11.3.4.1.b.

\(H = G(2)\text{.}\) We take any element of \(G(2)\) of maximal order. That is, consider \(\langle 8 \rangle = \{1, 8, 29, 22\}\text{.}\) For \(H'\) we need a 2-element subgroup generated by an element not in \(\langle 8\rangle\text{.}\) So let \(H' = \{1, 6\}\text{.}\) Again, the intersection is clearly just \(\{1\}\) and multiplying the elements of \(\langle 8 \rangle\) by \(6\) gives the other elements of \(H\text{.}\)
