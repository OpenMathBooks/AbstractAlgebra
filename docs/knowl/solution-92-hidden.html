An ideal is a type of subring (which is a subset that is also a ring). While a ring must be closed under addition, subtraction (i.e., additive inverses), and multiplication, and ideal must also have the property that the product of an element inside the ideal with any element of the ring (even outside the idea) will be an element in the idea. That is, \(ra \in I\) whenever \(r \in R\) and \(a \in I\text{,}\) the ideal.
