Suppose \(ghg\inv \in H\) for all \(g \in G\) and \(h \in H\text{.}\) Consider a fixed \(g \in G\text{.}\) We want to show that \(gH = Hg\text{,}\) which we will do by showing that everything in \(gH\) is already in \(Hg\text{,}\) and visa-versa.

First, let \(x \in gH\text{.}\) This means that \(x = gh_1\) for some \(h_1 \in H\text{.}\) But then \(x = gh_1(g\inv g) = (gh_1g\inv)g\text{.}\) Since \(gh_1g\inv \in H\text{,}\) we have that \(x \in Hg\text{.}\)

Similarly, if \(y \in Hg\text{,}\) then \(y = h_2g\) for some \(h_2 \in H\text{.}\) We have \(y = (gg\inv)h_2g = g(g\inv hg)\text{.}\) But \(g\inv h g \in H\) (as \(g\inv \in G\text{,}\) we have \((g\inv)h(g\inv)\inv \in H\)), so \(y \in gH\text{.}\)
