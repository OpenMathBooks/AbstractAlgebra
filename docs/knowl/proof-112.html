Proof

We will use mathematical induction on the degree of \(p(x)\text{.}\) If \(\deg p(x) = 1\text{,}\) then \(p(x)\) is a linear polynomial and \(E = F\text{.}\) Assume that the theorem is true for all polynomials of degree \(k\) with \(1 \leq k \lt n\) and let \(\deg p(x) = n\text{.}\) We can assume that \(p(x)\) is irreducible; otherwise, by our induction hypothesis, we are done. By TheoremÂ 8.13, there exists a field \(K\) such that \(p(x)\) has a zero \(\alpha_1\) in \(K\text{.}\) Hence, \(p(x) = (x - \alpha_1)q(x)\text{,}\) where \(q(x) \in K[x]\text{.}\) Since \(\deg q(x) = n -1\text{,}\) there exists a splitting field \(E \supset K\) of \(q(x)\) that contains the zeros \(\alpha_2, \ldots, \alpha_n\) of \(p(x)\) by our induction hypothesis. Consequently,

\begin{equation*} E = K(\alpha_2, \ldots, \alpha_n) = F(\alpha_1, \ldots, \alpha_n) \end{equation*}

is a splitting field of \(p(x)\text{.}\)
