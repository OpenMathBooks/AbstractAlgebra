The conjuages of \(r_0\) are all \(r_0\text{,}\) since \(gr_0g\inv = gg\inv = r_0\) for any \(g \in G\text{.}\) It is easy to check that the conjugates of \(r_2\) are allways \(r_2\text{.}\) Let's find some conjugates of \(d_1\text{:}\)

\begin{align*} r_0d_1r_0\inv \amp = d_1 \\ r_1d_1r_1\inv \amp = d_2 \\ r_2d_1r_2\inv \amp = d_1 \\ r_3d_1r_3\inv \amp = d_2 \\ hd_1h\inv \amp = d_2 \\ vd_1v\inv \amp = d_2 \\ d_1d_1d_1\inv \amp = d_1 \\ d_2d_1d_2\inv \amp = d_1 \text{.} \end{align*}

Similarly, the conjugates of \(d_2\) will be either \(d_1\) or \(d_2\text{.}\)

Note that the conjugates for things in \(H\) are always (possibly different) things in \(H\text{.}\) We say that \(H\) is closed under conjugates. Of course we knew this would happen since \(H\) is normal in \(G\text{.}\)
