If \(a,b \in G\) and \(ab = ba\text{,}\) we say that \(a\) and \(b\) commute. Assuming that \(a\) and \(b\) commute, prove the following.

  1. \(a\inv\) and \(b\inv\) commute.

  2. \(a\) and \(b\inv\) commute.

  3. \(a\) commutes with \(ab\text{.}\)

  4. \(a^2\) commutes with \(b^2\text{.}\)

  5. \(xax\inv\) commutes with \(xbx\inv\) for any \(x \in G\text{.}\)
