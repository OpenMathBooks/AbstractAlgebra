Exercise 1.

Suppose \(p(x)\) and \(q(x)\) are polynomials in \(R[x]\) where \(R\) is a commutative ring with unity. We want to understand how the degree of \(p(x) + q(x)\) and \(p(x)q(x)\) relate to the degrees of \(p(x)\) and \(q(x)\text{.}\)

  1. Give an example of polynomials \(p(x)\) and \(q(x)\) in \(\Z[x]\) so that \(\deg(p(x) + q(x)) \lt \max(\deg p(x),\deg q(x))\text{.}\)

  2. Give an example of polynomials \(p(x)\) and \(q(x)\) in \(\Z_{12}[x]\) so that \(\deg(p(x)q(x)) \lt \deg p(x) + \deg q(x)\text{.}\)

  3. Can you repeat the previous part if \(p(x)\) and \(q(x)\) were polynomials in \(\Z_{11}[x]\text{?}\) Explain.

  4. What can you say in general? In particular, in what situations can you claim that \(\deg(p(x)+q(x)) = \max(\deg p(x), \deg q(x))\) and in what situations can you claim that \(\deg(p(x)q(x)) = \deg p(x) + \deg q(x)\text{?}\)
