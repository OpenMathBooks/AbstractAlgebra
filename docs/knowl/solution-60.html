Solution 6.4.4.4.1.

We start by checking for roots (there are only 5 possibilities in \(\Z_5\)). We find that \(2\text{,}\) \(3\text{,}\) and \(4\) are roots. Dividing by the corresponding factors gives us

\begin{equation*} (x-2)(x-3)(x-4)(x^2 + 2) = (x+3)(x+2)(x+1)(x^2+2). \end{equation*}
