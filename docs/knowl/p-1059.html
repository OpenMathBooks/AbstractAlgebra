Define \(f:U(5) \to U(10)\) by \(f = \begin{pmatrix} 1 \amp 2 \amp 3 \amp 4 \\ 1 \amp 3 \amp 7 \amp 9\end{pmatrix}\text{.}\) By inspection, \(f\) is a bijection. To check that it is an isomorphism, we need to check the homomorphism property \(f(xy) = f(x)f(y)\text{.}\) This means checking lots of pairs. For example, \(ff(2\cdot 3) = f(1) = 1 = 3\cdot 7 = f(2)f(3)\text{.}\) You can also note that \(U(5)\) is cyclic with generator \(2\text{,}\) and \(U(10)\) is cyclic with generator \(3\text{,}\) so sending \(2\) to \(3\) will determine the isomorphism.
