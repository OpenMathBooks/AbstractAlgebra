Exercise 1.

Consider the number \(\alpha = \sqrt{3}+\sqrt{5}\) and the field \(\Q(\alpha)\text{,}\) a simple extension of \(\Q\text{.}\)

  1. Prove that \(\alpha\) is an algebraic number.

  2. Is \(\Q(\alpha)\) an extension field of \(\Q(\sqrt{3})\text{?}\) Is it an extension field of \(\Q(\sqrt{5})\text{?}\) Explain.

  3. If we take \(\Q(\sqrt{3})\) and adjoin \(\sqrt{5}\text{,}\) do we get a bigger field? We can write \(\Q(\sqrt{3})(\sqrt{5})\) as \(\Q(\sqrt{3},\sqrt{5})\text{.}\)

  4. Find the minimal polynomials for \(\sqrt{3}\) over \(\Q\) and of \(\alpha = \sqrt{3}+\sqrt{5}\) of \(\Q(\sqrt{3})\text{.}\) How do the degrees of these relate to the degree of the polynomial you found for \(\alpha\) in part (a)?
