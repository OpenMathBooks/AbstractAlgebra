Exercise 1.

Consider the field \(\Q(\sqrt[4]{3}, i)\text{.}\)

  1. Is this a splitting field for some polynomial in \(\Q[x]\text{?}\) If so, what is the degree of that polynomial.

  2. What is the \([Q(\sqrt[4]{3}, i):\Q]\text{?}\) Explain how you know.

  3. It turns out that there are exactly 8 different fields between \(\Q\) and \(\Q(\sqrt[4]{3}, i)\text{.}\) Find at least four of these (more if you can) and draw a large tower diagram showing how these are related (with degrees marked appropriately).

  4. Bonus! (but not really that hard, just not something we have done in a while): Prove that the fields \(\Q(\sqrt[4]{3})\) and \(\Q(\sqrt[4]{3}i)\) are isomorphic, but not equal. Note, this fact might be helpful for the previous parts.
