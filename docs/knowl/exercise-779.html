Exercise 1.

Let \(G\) be the additive group of real numbers, \((\R,+)\) and let \(X = \R^2\) be the real plane. For each point \(P \in X\) and each \(r \in G\text{,}\) define \((r,P)\) to be the point \(P\) lands on after rotating the plane counterclockwise about the origin through \(r\) radians.

  1. Prove that \(X\) is a \(G\)-set. That is, prove that the procedure above defines an action of \(G\) on \(X\text{.}\)

  2. Pick a point \(P\) other than the origin. Describe geometrically the orbit containing \(P\text{.}\) That is, find \(\mathcal O_P\text{.}\)

  3. Find the stabilizer subgroup of \(P\) (i.e., the group \(G_P\)) for the point \(P\) you picked above.

  4. Consider the group elements \(g = \pi\) and \(h = 2\pi\text{.}\) Find the fixed points sets \(X_g\) and \(X_h\text{.}\) Are there any other fixed point sets for other choices of group elements? Briefly explain.
